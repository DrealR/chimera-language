# Living Questions

A living question is not engagement bait. It is a bounded unknown with enough source lineage, openness, and consequence to let distinct receivers add structure from their own walked paths.

## Language function

A question performs several operations at once:

1. separates known from unknown;
2. names the contour of the gap;
3. directs attention without requiring one answer;
4. gives another body an entry role;
5. creates an address where later responses can attach.

> **A statement places an object in the field. A question places an open relation in the field.**

## Five forms

- **Mirror:** use one familiar system to reveal another.
- **Seam:** locate where two explanations stop fitting.
- **Temporal:** let different life stages or eras work on one object.
- **Operator:** expose where direction, veto, or weighting resides.
- **Earth:** ask what turns activity into memory, capacity, relationship, and generation.

## Translation discipline

Before publishing, record:

- the lived observation that earned the question;
- the smallest plain-language skeleton;
- the intended receiver routes;
- what kind of response could change the model;
- which answer the wording accidentally privileges;
- the carrier image and where it stops;
- how a response could return to embodied practice;
- the source path and later corrections.

## Question test

A question is healthier when it:

- is open but inhabitable;
- asks for proof of passage rather than status performance;
- permits disagreement and counterexample;
- can travel across dialects without losing boundaries or uncertainty;
- remains meaningful after the current trend;
- may produce action or an independent center;
- does not extract intimacy merely to generate visibility.

The human remains the final weighting operator. AI may retrieve seams, generate variants, translate, and find related receivers, but it should return a finite deck and ask:

> **Which question is alive for you today?**
