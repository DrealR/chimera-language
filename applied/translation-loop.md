# The CHIMERA Translation Loop

```text
Ground → Name → Trace carrier → Reflect → Translate → Classify
       → Delete → Restore → Practice → Teach back → Entangle
```

1. **Ground:** learn the receiver's body, domain, history, channel, and live question.
2. **Name:** give the smallest plain, checkable skeleton.
3. **Trace carrier:** distinguish physical signal, transduction, encoding, medium, decoding, interpretation, and what may be lost.
4. **Reflect:** generate two or three candidate mirrors from familiar domains.
5. **Translate:** choose the mirror with lived traction and preserve the structural invariants.
6. **Classify:** mark mechanism, practice, observation, carrier, formal claim, or speculation.
7. **Delete:** remove CHIMERA vocabulary. If nothing useful survives, the translation only renamed.
8. **Restore:** return story, beauty, cultural language, and dignity after the skeleton is visible.
9. **Practice:** let the claim pass through a real body with feedback and consequence.
10. **Teach back:** the receiver expresses it through their own domain and supplies a counterexample.
11. **Entangle:** preserve source, artifact, losses, limits, response, and later correction.

Translation succeeds when the other body gains a usable distinction or capacity—not merely when it repeats the words.
