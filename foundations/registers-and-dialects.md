# Registers and Dialects

Translation requires two coordinates that earlier CHIMERA language often collapsed.

## Register: what kind of claim is this?

1. **Operator / mechanism** — bounded causal or practical claim at a named scale.
2. **Carrier / story** — memorable image that preserves roles, sequence, or tension.
3. **Formal / domain** — claim governed by a discipline's definitions and evidence.
4. **Source / cosmology** — metaphysics and developmental synthesis preserved as lineage.

## Dialect: how can this receiver metabolize it?

Child/coach, plain, artist/story, builder/AI, teacher, expert/skeptic, and CHIMERA-native are different receiver routes. They do not create different evidence standards.

> **Register changes wording. Classification controls what wording is allowed to claim.**

A basketball story may carry a grounded operator. A physics-sounding sentence may carry speculation. Technical vocabulary is not a rigor badge.

## The invariance test

Across a good translation, preserve:

- bodies and boundaries;
- direction of flow;
- sequence of transformation;
- operator, veto, and stop condition;
- cost, risk, and residue;
- uncertainty and the point where the mapping stops.

If only emotional shape transfers, call it a carrier. If causal structure transfers, state it plainly. If it becomes a formal claim, test it in the host domain.
