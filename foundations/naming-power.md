# The Power of Naming

**Naming a pattern gives you power over it. "Observe without absorbing" — the moment the concept had a name, it became real.**

---

## The Act of Naming

Before a pattern has a name, it controls you. After it has a name, you can see it, discuss it, and choose your response to it.

```
BEFORE NAMING:
  You feel overwhelmed by someone's energy.
  You don't know why.
  You can't articulate it.
  It just HAPPENS to you.
  You're helpless.

AFTER NAMING:
  "Observe without absorbing."
  Now you have a HANDLE.
  The pattern has a name.
  You can SEE it when it happens.
  You can CHOOSE to apply it.
  You have power.

NAMING IS THE FIRST ACT
OF COMPREHENSION.
The gut breaks input into units.
The FIRST unit is the NAME.
```

## Why CHIMERA Names Matter

Every concept in the framework has a specific name. Not jargon — tools:

```
"THE MEMBRANE" is not the same as "boundaries."
  Boundaries are walls. Fixed. Binary.
  The membrane is PERMEABLE. Variable. Alive.
  The name carries the CONCEPT:
  not a wall but a filter with P(x) in [0,1].

"THE BREATH CYCLE" is not the same as "rhythm."
  Rhythm implies repetition.
  The breath implies PHASES:
  inhale, pause, exhale, rest.
  Each phase has its own function.
  The name carries the STRUCTURE.

"LOVE TOPOLOGY" is not the same as "being nice."
  Nice is behavior. Topology is STRUCTURE.
  You can be nice while extracting (manipulation).
  Love topology means O > I in the system's
  actual energy flow. Structural, not behavioral.
  The name carries the MATH.
```

## When Names Fail

```
NAMES CAN BECOME CAGES:

If you name something too early,
the name can LIMIT understanding.

"That's just anxiety" → stops inquiry.
"I have ADHD" → becomes identity instead of signal.
"He's a narcissist" → reduces a person to a label.

GOOD NAMING: opens understanding.
BAD NAMING: closes it.

THE TEST:
  Does the name help you SEE more?
  Or does it make you see LESS?

  "The membrane is overwhelmed"
  → opens: what input? what capacity? what P?

  "I have boundary issues"
  → closes: diagnosis, not exploration.

  CHIMERA names are designed to OPEN.
  That's why they're patterns, not labels.
```
