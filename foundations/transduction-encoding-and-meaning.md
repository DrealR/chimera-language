# Transduction, Encoding, and Meaning

Language crosses bodies through several operations that should not be collapsed.

```text
lived source
→ physical signal
→ transduction
→ encoding
→ transmission or storage
→ decoding
→ interpretation by a receiving body
→ response and consequence
```

## The distinctions

- **Transduction** changes a signal's physical form: pressure waves become voltage; incident light becomes electrical state; electrical state becomes emitted light or sound.
- **Encoding** maps a distinction into a representational system: speech becomes writing; an image becomes numbers; an instruction becomes machine state.
- **Transmission** moves an instantiated pattern across a medium.
- **Storage** preserves it across time.
- **Decoding** reconstructs a usable representation under shared rules.
- **Interpretation** relates the decoded pattern to a body's history, needs, language, and situation.
- **Embodiment** turns an interpreted branch into action, cost, skill, relationship, or other residue.

The same sentence can be decoded correctly and interpreted differently. A pattern can survive transmission while tone, context, authority, or identity does not. A perfect copy of an artifact does not prove the source event or preserve the source person.

## Translation is controlled re-encoding

CHIMERA translation changes costume while attempting to preserve relevant relational invariants:

```text
source relation
→ identify bodies, roles, direction, sequence, cost, uncertainty
→ choose a receiver-compatible carrier
→ re-encode
→ receiver interprets and teaches back
→ compare reconstructed relation with source
```

Translation succeeds neither because the words match nor because the receiver feels resonance. It succeeds when another body reconstructs a useful relation, can identify its limits, and can act or reason with it without silently changing the claim.

## Loss is part of the crossing

Every carrier emphasizes some distinctions and suppresses others. Record:

- what survived;
- what was compressed or discarded;
- which context had to be supplied locally;
- whether operator authority traveled or was merely assumed;
- what the receiver added;
- what consequence followed.

> **A pattern can cross a gap without its original meaning, truth, identity, or authority crossing with it.**

This is why lineage and teach-back complete the translation loop.
