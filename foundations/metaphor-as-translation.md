## Metaphor Is Not Decoration

The traditional view: metaphor is literary ornamentation. Pretty language. Optional.

The cognitive science view (Lakoff & Johnson, 1980): metaphor is how the brain THINKS. Abstract thought is built ON concrete experience. You can't think about love without thinking about journeys, warmth, containers, forces. Every abstract concept is grounded in bodily metaphor.

```
TRADITIONAL VIEW:
  "Love is a journey" = poetry.
  Nice but unnecessary.
  The real meaning is literal.

COGNITIVE VIEW:
  "Love is a journey" = cognition.
  You LITERALLY think about love
  using the neural pathways
  for physical travel.

  "We're going nowhere."
  "This relationship has baggage."
  "We've come a long way."

  These aren't decorations.
  These are how the brain
  PROCESSES the concept.
```

## Body as Source Domain

Lakoff's key insight: nearly ALL abstract concepts are grounded in body experience.

```
BODY EXPERIENCE → ABSTRACT CONCEPT:

Physical warmth → emotional warmth
  "A warm person." "A cold shoulder."

Physical balance → fairness
  "That's unbalanced." "Level the playing field."

Physical sight → understanding
  "I see what you mean." "That's unclear."

Physical grasp → comprehension
  "I can't grasp this." "Let me hold that thought."

Physical digestion → processing
  "I can't digest this." "Let me chew on that."

THE BODY IS THE FIRST LANGUAGE.
All other language is built on it.

That's why chimera-body was built first.
It's the SOURCE DOMAIN for everything.
```

## Why Metaphors Can Be Wrong

```
NOT ALL METAPHORS ARE VALID.

A metaphor fails when
the structural mapping breaks down.

"The brain is a computer":
  PARTIAL TRUTH — both process information.
  FAILS — computers don't feel,
    don't have bodies,
    don't grow through experience.
  The mapping is SHALLOW.

"The gut is a black hole":
  DEEP TRUTH — both digest to fundamental units,
    both have autonomous intelligence,
    both communicate bidirectionally
    with larger system.
  The mapping is STRUCTURAL.

THE TEST: Does the metaphor make
  PREDICTIONS that turn out true?

  "Gut = black hole" predicts:
  black holes should have a "microbiome"
  (diverse processing entities).
  IF Hawking radiation reveals diversity:
  the metaphor predicted physics.
  That's not decoration. That's science.
```
